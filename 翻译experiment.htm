ex‧per‧i‧ment

▶ noun /ikˈsperəmənt/ ♫ a scientific procedure undertaken to make a discovery, test a hypothesis, or demonstrate a known fact: laboratory experiments on guinea pigs | I have tested this by experiment.

• a course of action tentatively adopted without being sure of the eventual outcome: the previous experiment in liberal democracy had ended in disaster.

▶ verb /ikˈsperəˌment/ ♫ [no obj.] perform a scientific procedure, esp. in a laboratory, to determine something: she experimented on chickens as well as mice.

• try out new concepts or ways of doing things: the designers experimented with new ideas in lighting.

→ ex‧per‧i‧men‧ta‧tion /ikˌsperəmənˈtāshən/ noun,

→ ex‧per‧i‧ment‧er noun

– origin Middle English: from Old French, or from Latin experimentum, from experiri ‘try.’ Compare with experience and expert.

此外，冥想其实也不是"专注于不想什么"。因为人类大脑并不能理解否定词“不”，你越让它不想什么，它就越想什么。它把你的指令分成”不“和“想某物”两部分，前半部分无法运行，所以只运行后半部分。理论来源详见Daniel M. Wegner在1987年做的白熊实验（white bear experiment）。后来这个实验写成了一本书White Bears and Other Unwanted Thoughts: Suppression, Obsession, and the Psychology of Mental Control: Daniel M. Wegner: 9780898622232: Books。 


冥想（meditation）这个词是印地语vipassana的意译，在翻译过程中出现了含义的曲解。vipassana的本来意思是坐在那里，引导注意力长时间观察自己的呼吸（或其他简单目标），从而让大脑摆脱无时不在的焦虑思绪，实现内心和身体的平静。所以从含义来看，“内观”或“静坐”是比较准确的翻译。【观心中的沧海波涛】


meditation在英语里的原意是“沉思、深入思考”，汉译的“冥想”其实是对英语meditation的准确直译。但因为从印地语到英语的翻译中出现了信息丢失和扭曲，所以汉译也跟着奇怪起来，出现了错误的暗示。


BTW，关于Vipassana的本质和技术细节，有很多渠道可以了解。比如看书，硅谷最受欢迎的情商课 (豆瓣)。有条件可以直接参加10天的内观课程，Vipassana Meditation。

  • Arms
  • Clinical trials can include multiple "arms." Each arm is a study group of patients receiving a specific treatment or combination of treatments that is being compared to other treatment arms as well as to the control arm. The "control arm" is the best available standard treatment.
  • 或者
  • Arm
  • [In a controlled trial.] Refers to a group of participants allocated a particular treatment. In a randomised controlled trial, allocation to different arms is determined by the randomisation procedure. Many controlled trials have two arms, a group of participants assigned to an experimental intervention (sometimes called the treatment arm) and a group of participants assigned to a control (the control arm). Trials may have more than two arms, with more than one experimental arm and/or more than one control arm.
